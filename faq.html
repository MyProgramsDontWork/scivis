Frequently Asked Questions


What is the USSRC?

The USSRC (United States Space & Rocket Center) is the location of all of our programs which is part of the rocket center foundation and is owned by the Alabama state government and affilated with NASA and the Smithsonian.


How does the SCIVIS Week compare to a regular week at Space Camp?

This special week is coordinated by teachers of the visually impaired from all over the United States. Many adaptations are available this week that are not available during a normal week at Space Camp. Braille, large print, CCTV's, voice output on selected computers, visually impaired speakers, and support help from certified teachers of the visually impaired are just a few. Discounted tuition is also available this week as a result of the group rate.


Can my child's non visually impaired sibilings or friends come to SCIVIS?

Siblings or friends of visually impaired students are welcome to attend the same week.


Why should my child attend SCIVIS?

SCIVIS is perfect for self-sufficient students with interest in math and science who want to meet new people, have fun, and learn as it allows them to do all of those things. So, if you're child checks all these boxes they would enjoy SCIVIS.


What does program cost cover?

Program costs which are found on the home page cover all activites, housing, and food. Program costs do not cover snacks from the planetarium or vending machines, gift shop purchases, or transportation.


Is assistance in making travel arrangements available?

For travel assistance we use the USSRC's official partner ADTRAV. If you decide to contact ADTRAV please have date of departure, date of arrival, city of deprature, city of arrival (Huntsville, Alabama), the program the traveler is attending (Space Camp, Aviation Challenge, etc), and credit card info.


Please note that most campers are eligable for airport assistance for acessibility and solo travelers under the age of 15 must book flights as UMs (Unacompanied Minors) for additional charge.


Can parents come to camp?

Parents are able to come for camp graduation on the Thursday of SCIVIS week around 7 P.M. and stay at hotels near 1 Tranquility Base. Parents are also able to do activites at the USSRC that are open to the public. Parents are not able to take their kids out of camp or enter camp only areas unless granted permission by camper services.


Can my child come to camp early or leave later?

Campers are allowed to come as early as 11 A.M. on the Saturday of SCIVIS week at a charge of $150 per day. Campers can leave from after graduation to the end of Friday.


What are my options for airport transportation?

Parents are able to drive their children to and from the aiport. Additionally a shuttle service is avialable for campers at the price of $75 round trip. Campers using the shuttle will be met at their gate, taken to get their bags, and get on the bus returning they will have their bags taken off the bus, check bags, go through TSA, have the option to get food or souvenirs, and get handed over to the airline.


How much spending money should I send with my child?

The amount of money being sent is up to you, but campers are taken to a gift shop twice, a snack bar once, and will have the option to use vending machines many times. The USSRC offers wristbands that can be used at the gift shop and snack bar, and filled through mission control. Campers are able to bring cash, credit cards, or debit cards, we reccomend cash or wristbands for younger campers as they limit spending. Campers can keep money in lockers in their rooms or with their chaperones, but can't keep them with USSRC staff.


What if my child has dietary needs?

We can accomadate dietary needs as long as we know of them two weeks ahead of time. Let us know if you have dietary needs at specialdiets@spacecamp.com.


What if my child takes medication?

We can adminster medication as long as given notice through the manditory health form. Students are required to keep a week's worth of medication in the sick bay where we have a registered nurse avaible 24/7. Students should continute to take any medications that effect behavior or ability to learn


What if my child requires medical attention>

If medical attention is needed we can provide medical aid on site, and any emergencies are sent to the Huntsville Hospital for Women and Children.

What are the sleeping arrangements?

Trainees (campers) stay in dormitories called Habitats. Aviation Challenge rooms are similar to military barracks whereas Space Camp programs are more so like large dorm rooms. Placement in the habitat is determined by our availability. The beds are bunk beds, and a bottom bed may be requested for medical reasons, sleepwalking, etc. Males and females are housed separately.


How secure are the premises?

The premises have uniformed guards on site 24/7. Additionally the habitats house the RAs and Crew Trainers (Group Leaders).


How many other people will be at camp?

Trainees will be placed on a team with 12 – 16 others. They will be with that team the entire duration of camp. The total number of trainees for SCIVIS week usually is about 170 to 200 trainees with approximately 60-75 vision professionals in attendance, as well.


Who are crew trainers?

Our team leaders, are known as Crew Trainers, come from all backgrounds and walks of life. While one year of college is required, many have completed their degrees. Crew Trainers are background checked during the hiring process. They must complete a lengthy training which includes instruction in curriculum, discipline and safety.


How does the USSRC handle bullying?

Our crew trainers first attempt to desculate, then give a warning, then send students to managment, then suspend students from activites for 1 day, then decamp students.
